- Making money (overview): there's no single "get rich" button in Torn - big networth comes from stacking several income streams over time. The main legit ways to make cash are: owning/working a company, the stock market, bank interest, item running (travel trading), crimes and mugging, hunting, racing, and odds and ends like bounties, looting, and the museum. [source: Torn money-making basics | 2026-07-17]
- Making money - companies: owning a high-tier company is one of the best passive earners once it's built up - the top money companies are the Oil Rig, Logistics Management, Television Network, and Private Security Firm. Even just working for a good company pays a salary plus valuable job-point specials. [source: Torn money-making basics | 2026-07-17]
- Making money - stocks: buy low and sell high, and hold big share blocks for recurring cash dividends. The biggest cash payouts are Crude & Co (CNC, ~$80m/month at 7.5m shares) and Torn & Shanghai Banking (TSB, ~$50m/month at 3m shares), with smaller ones like TC Music ($25m/month), Insured On Us ($12m/month), Grain ($4m/month), and the Torn City Times ($1m/month). [source: Torn money-making basics | 2026-07-17]
- Making money - banking & interest: park cash in the bank for interest (longer terms pay more), and boost it with the Torn City Investments stock (TCI, +10% interest) and the Cayman offshore bank (via the faction Offshore Banking perk). It's slow but safe compounding on large balances. [source: Torn money-making basics | 2026-07-17]
- Making money - item running (travel trading): the classic earner. Fly abroad, buy items that are cheap overseas - plushies, flowers, and drugs (Xanax especially) - and resell them in Torn for a markup. Bigger travel capacity (suitcases, Cruise Line/Lingerie perks, stocks) means more profit per flight. [source: Torn money-making basics | 2026-07-17]
- Making money - crimes & combat: solo Crimes 2.0 (theft, etc.) pay out cash, and faction Organized Crimes are a major money source - the biggest OCs pay tens of millions and up. In combat you can Mug opponents to steal 5-15% of the cash they're carrying. [source: Torn money-making basics | 2026-07-17]
- Making money - hunting & racing: hunting in South Africa at high skill can pull roughly $800k per full energy bar (donator). Racing pays through the Ultimate Racing Tournament (URT) - its grand prize is $1 billion plus a car - and through betting on custom races. [source: Torn money-making basics | 2026-07-17]
- Making money - odds and ends: collect bounties placed on other players, loot NPCs (Duke, Leslie, Scrooge) for cash and rare items, and exchange full plushie/flower sets at the museum for points (which sell for cash). The casino can pay but it's gambling, not a reliable income. Note: The Duke is the NPC loan shark you BORROW from (up to $2b) - he's not an income source, and you can't become him. [source: Torn money-making basics | 2026-07-17]
- Networth scale (for reference): $50 billion is a serious fortune. Networth medals run Millionaire ($1m), Billionaire ($1b), Multibillionaire ($2.5b), Baron ($10b), Oligarch ($25b), and Tycoon ($100b) - so $50b sits between Oligarch and Tycoon. A Dirty Bomb and a unique property are each valued around $50b for networth. [source: Torn money-making basics | 2026-07-17]
