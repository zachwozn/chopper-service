- Trade (overview): Torn has player-to-player direct trading. You open a trade two ways: Sidebar > Items > Trades (top right) > enter a User ID/Name > add a description > Initiate; or from a Player Profile > Actions > Trade > description > Initiate. Both sides add or remove items until they accept; then the trade "locks" and is finalized by the Initiator. [source: https://wiki.torn.com/wiki/Trade | 2026-07-18]
- Trade - what you can trade: always tradeable are Money, Items, and Properties. Also tradeable but only if you hold the role: Companies (only if you're the Director) and Factions (only if you're the leader). [source: https://wiki.torn.com/wiki/Trade | 2026-07-18]
- Trade - what you CANNOT trade: Stocks/shares, Points, Merits, Rackets, Territories, Books, and any stats or statuses are all non-tradeable. (Shares used to be tradeable but that was removed with the Stocks 3.0 update on 6 April 2021.) [source: https://wiki.torn.com/wiki/Trade | 2026-07-18]
- Trade - process & safety: either party can add/remove elements via the '+' by each category until acceptance. Once the other party accepts, the trade is "locked" and contents can't change on either side; the Initiator then gives final confirmation to complete it. Every 'Accept' has a 5-second re-confirmation timer so both sides can review first (a player-safe protocol; some scripts can remove the re-confirmation, which is not recommended). A trade can be cancelled any time before the Initiator's final confirm, returning all contents to their owners. [source: https://wiki.torn.com/wiki/Trade | 2026-07-18]
- Trade - features: traded elements count toward your "pending" networth. Both parties get events at each stage (initiated, accepted, completed). You can add comments (max 155 characters, once per minute) and commenting pings the other user (with spam limits). Added elements are visible to the other party even before they accept. You can put any amount of money into a trade. [source: https://wiki.torn.com/wiki/Trade | 2026-07-18]
- Ghost trade: a trade you initiate with no intention of completing, used to "hide" money - money sitting in an open trade cannot be mugged off you. It's usually done with a trusted friend or faction mate. Caution: a ghost trade can be cancelled and, once cancelled, the money returns to your hand immediately and IS muggable again - so the other party could cancel and then mug you if they wanted. [source: https://wiki.torn.com/wiki/Trade | 2026-07-18]
- Trade - limitations: you can trade from hospital and jail but NOT while flying/abroad. A single trade can move at most 10,000 total elements from one side. A trade lasts up to 6 hours; if not completed it "times out" and contents return to owners after a 15-minute gap. Money from a cancel or time-out reaches you immediately and is muggable (whereas money mid-trade is safe). To trade a company the buyer must join it; to trade a faction the buyer must join and wait 72 hours for Recruit Status to end. To trade a property you must first empty its vault, move out, and fire all its staff. [source: https://wiki.torn.com/wiki/Trade | 2026-07-18]
