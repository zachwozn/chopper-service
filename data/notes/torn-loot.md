- Loot / Looting (overview): looting is when groups of players team up to attack NPCs (non-player characters) for special loot - items like unique clothing and weapons only obtainable this way. Each lootable NPC has a "loot level" from 1 to 5; the loot level at the moment the NPC is defeated sets how many players receive loot, and higher loot levels also give better chances at rarer items. On defeat the loot level resets to 1, then rises again at 30, 90, 210, and 450 minutes after that defeat (shown on the status bar, e.g. "Loot Level III"). [source: https://wiki.torn.com/wiki/Loot | 2026-07-18]
- Loot - lottery mechanics: loot is awarded lottery-style to participants, weighted by effort/damage - you don't have to land the final hit or even be in the fight at the moment of defeat to win loot. To earn a loot ticket you must either land a damaging hit or successfully land a temporary item on the NPC. Notes: DOT (damage-over-time) counts as your own damage; multiple hits in one turn don't give extra tickets; non-damaging hits give no ticket; and tickets expire when the NPC is defeated or regens to full life. NPCs are looted immediately on defeat (no Mug/Hospitalize option). [source: https://wiki.torn.com/wiki/Loot | 2026-07-18]
- Loot - lootable NPCs (overview): there are 13 lootable NPCs, 8 of them seasonal. 5 are available all year (Duke, Leslie, Jimmy, Fernando, Tiny), 2 are seasonal singles (Scrooge in December, Easter Bunny in April), and 6 appear only during Halloween week (M'aol and his 5 praetorians), and only once 9,999 treats have been used to summon M'aol. [source: https://wiki.torn.com/wiki/Loot | 2026-07-18]
