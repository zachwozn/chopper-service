- Gym (overview): you spend energy at the gym to train Strength, Speed, Defense, and Dexterity. Every train also costs happy - you lose about 40-60% of the energy used as happiness. There are 33 gyms total: 24 standard (progressing up to George's) plus 9 specialist gyms. You unlock better gyms by earning gym EXP through training. New players start at Premier Fitness. [source: https://wiki.torn.com/wiki/Gym | 2026-07-17]
- Gym - standard progression: Lightweight gyms use 5 energy/train (Premier Fitness 2.0 dots, Global Gym 4.0). Middle/Heavyweight gyms use 10 energy/train, climbing to George's - the final standard gym at 7.3 dots on all stats (no EXP gained past it). More "dots" (multiplier bars) = bigger gains. [source: https://wiki.torn.com/wiki/Gym | 2026-07-17]
- Gym - specialist gyms: unlocked after George's with certain stat distributions. Balboas (Defense/Dexterity), Frontline Fitness (Strength/Speed), Gym 3000 (Strength only, 50 energy, 8.0 dots), The Sports Science Lab (balanced 9.0 all stats, capped at 150 Xanax/Ecstasy combined), and the invite-only Fight Club. [source: https://wiki.torn.com/wiki/Gym | 2026-07-17]
- Gym - gains factors: your gain per train depends on happy (higher happy = much bigger gains), the gym's dots, energy per train, your current stat total, and modifiers (faction Steadfast perks, Sports Science education, company bonuses). Happy is the single biggest lever, which is why people stack happy (candy, Ecstasy, eDVDs) before training. [source: https://wiki.torn.com/wiki/Gym | 2026-07-17]
- Gym - books & jail gym: 30-day books boost gains - stat-specific books +30% for that stat, "Get Hard Or Go Home" +20% all gains, "Higher Daddy, Higher!" +20% energy regen. While jailed you can only use the Crims gym (5 energy/train, ~3.4 str/speed, 4.5 defense - better defense gains than lightweight gyms for new players). [source: https://wiki.torn.com/wiki/Gym | 2026-07-17]
