- Jail (overview): you get jailed by failing a crime (red result), failing a bust, failing an OC with a jail result, or being arrested by another player. Jail time runs from ~10 minutes to several hours. While jailed you can train at the Crims gym (no dexterity, but good strength/speed/defense). [source: https://wiki.torn.com/wiki/Jail | 2026-07-17]
- Jail - busting people out: costs 5 nerve per attempt (reducible to 2 via faction upgrades). Success depends on faction Criminality perks (up to +50%), Law education, Law Firm employment, how many busts you've done, and level (higher level busts better; lower-level targets are easier). Spamming busts back-to-back lowers your success. Three outcomes: success (frees them), failure (you just lose nerve), or you get jailed yourself (1-3 hours). [source: https://wiki.torn.com/wiki/Jail | 2026-07-17]
- Jail - self-bust & bail: busting yourself costs half your max nerve (rounded up), cut to a third with LAW2990 education; failing adds jail time. Bail cost = 100 x remaining jail minutes x inmate level. Bail is cheaper with Law education (up to ~57.25%) and a 1-star Law Firm special (50%). Common Law education unlocks bailing yourself out. [source: https://wiki.torn.com/wiki/Jail | 2026-07-17]
- Jail - helpful bust tools & CE loss: to get out or bust better - Bachelor of Law makes busting 50% easier and buying-out 50% cheaper; a 5-star+ Law Firm helps (5-star "Closing Argument" allows longer bust chains, 10-star "Educated Decisions" shows bust success %); a Lawyer Business Card instantly frees you (consumed on use, from LAG stock benefit); and a Law-job Trial Lawyer can free a player for 15 job points. Note: your dexterity no longer affects bust chance. When you go to jail from a failed crime you can lose Crime Experience, but the loss is capped (at most ~20x what you'd have gained, and never more than 1% of your total CE). [source: https://wiki.torn.com/wiki/Jail | 2026-07-18]
