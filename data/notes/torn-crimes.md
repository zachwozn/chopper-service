- Crimes 2.0 (overview): Torn's solo crime system (separate from faction Organized Crimes). There are 13 crimes spread across 6 categories: Vandalism, Theft, Counterfeiting, Fraud, Illicit services, and Cybercrime. Each crime has a specific enhancer item that boosts your success/results for that crime, and many crimes have a large pool of possible outcomes. [source: Torn API (crimes) | 2026-07-17]
- Crimes 2.0 - full list of all 13 crimes (by type): Vandalism = Graffiti, Arson. Theft = Search for Cash, Shoplifting, Pickpocketing, Burglary. Counterfeiting = Bootlegging, Forgery. Fraud = Card Skimming, Hustling, Scamming. Illicit Services = Disposal. Cybercrime = Cracking. (That's 2 + 4 + 2 + 3 + 1 + 1 = 13.) [source: Torn API (crimes) | 2026-07-17]

- Crime enhancers (mechanic): each crime has one matching enhancer item that improves it - e.g. Glasses for Search for Cash, Mountain Bike for Shoplifting, Cut-Throat Razor for Pickpocketing, Flashlight for Burglary, Windproof Lighter for Arson, Paint Mask for Graffiti, Office Chair for Cracking, Latex Gloves for Disposal. [source: Torn API (crimes) | 2026-07-17]
- Crimes 2.0 - CE vs CS: every crime costs nerve. Success gives Crime Experience (CE) and Crime Skill (CS). CE is a hidden metric that sets your natural nerve bar (no cap, but each +5 is harder) and boosts your success in faction Organized Crimes - it's not tied to any one crime. CS is a per-crime level (each crime has its own skill bar). Crimes 2.0 went live 20 June 2023; new players are auto-enrolled since 22 Jan 2024, and migrating from the old system is one-way (you can't go back). [source: https://wiki.torn.com/wiki/Crimes_2.0 | 2026-07-17]
- Crimes 2.0 - subcrimes & outcomes: each crime has several subcrimes, some available at level 1 and others unlocked by getting a special item or by leveling that crime's skill. Three outcome types: Success (cash and/or items plus CE/CS), Fail (orange, no reward but no loss), and Critical Fail (red, big CE/CS loss - often dropping a level - plus possible jail, life loss, hospital time, item/money loss, or a crime-specific penalty like losing your enhancer). [source: https://wiki.torn.com/wiki/Crimes_2.0 | 2026-07-17]
- Crimes 2.0 - unique outcomes: some outcomes can only be gotten once ("uniquely"), often giving better rewards or a special item needed for later subcrimes (e.g. finding the Metal Detector in Search the Junkyard). They're marked by a star on the subcrime button. Some are skill-dependent (dropping a level via crit fail can lock you out) and some are time-dependent (e.g. only at high tide on the Beach). [source: https://wiki.torn.com/wiki/Crimes_2.0 | 2026-07-17]
- Crimes 2.0 - crime chain & progression: a crime chain builds up as you succeed at crimes (any crime), and a higher chain means bigger CE/CS gains on your next successes. A Fail halves your chain bonus; a Critical Fail zeroes it AND demoralizes you (reduced CE/CS gains until it wears off with more successes). Crime Progression bonuses stack additively: +10% faction Criminality, +10% Crime Progression merit, +10% education, +5% the crime's enhancer, +5% completing the Law starter job, and up to +15% from company specials. [source: https://wiki.torn.com/wiki/Crimes_2.0 | 2026-07-17]
- Crime "Search for Cash" (Theft, enhancer Glasses): search spots - Trash, Subway, Junkyard (available from the start), Beach (needs a Metal Detector), Cemetery (needs a Cemetery Key), and Fountain (needs crime skill 75). Success rates vary by time-dependent factors. 34 unique outcomes; a critical fail can lose you your Glasses enhancer. [source: https://wiki.torn.com/wiki/Crimes_2.0 | 2026-07-17]
- Crime "Bootlegging" (Counterfeiting, enhancer High-Speed Drive): copy DVDs (needs a PC/Laptop and Blank DVDs), sell counterfeit DVDs (needs copied DVDs in stock), and set up an online store (needs level 50, the CMT2230 Web Design education course, and a computer). 4 unique outcomes; a crit fail can lose your High-Speed Drive or PC. [source: https://wiki.torn.com/wiki/Crimes_2.0 | 2026-07-17]
- Crime "Graffiti" (Vandalism, enhancer Paint Mask): tag 7 districts - East Side, West Side, North Side, Residential, Red-Light, Financial, and City Centre - using spray paints (which get used up). Each area has 5 reputation stars earned by tagging; higher rep gives bigger cash and unique results, and rep decays over time as authorities cover it. Reputation is separate per area. 41 unique outcomes. [source: https://wiki.torn.com/wiki/Crimes_2.0 | 2026-07-17]
- Crime "Shoplifting" (Theft, enhancer Mountain Bike): hit 8 shops - Sally's Sweet Shop, Bits 'n' Bobs, TC Clothing, Super Store, Pharmacy, Cyber Force, Jewelry Store, and Big Al's Gun Shop. Each attempt raises your notoriety in that shop; each shop has security statuses that can toggle on/off affecting outcomes. A Torn City Times adds extra outcomes. 58 unique outcomes; a crit fail can lose your Mountain Bike. [source: https://wiki.torn.com/wiki/Crimes_2.0 | 2026-07-17]
- Crime "Pickpocketing" (Theft, enhancer Cut-Throat Razor): pickpocket unsuspecting pedestrians - no item needed. There are 22 mark types, all available at skill level 1, but some marks are much harder than others. 22 unique outcomes (one per mark). [source: https://wiki.torn.com/wiki/Crimes_2.0 | 2026-07-17]
- Crime "Card Skimming" (Fraud, enhancer Duct Tape): needs a Card Skimmer and a Spy Camera - install the skimmer on an ATM to slowly collect card details (camera catches the PIN), then recover it to collect and sell the details (both items returned on success). The longer you leave it, the higher the chance of losing an item. Max 20 active skimmers at once. 0 unique outcomes. [source: https://wiki.torn.com/wiki/Crimes_2.0 | 2026-07-17]
- Crime "Burglary" (Theft, enhancer Flashlight): scout locations (34 unique targets), optionally "case" one to raise your success chance, then burgle. A failed casing/burglary can make a property too dangerous to retry for a while. Each property has a recommended item that boosts the reward pool if you have it. Max 100 scouted properties; unused ones drop off. 34 unique outcomes (one per target). [source: https://wiki.torn.com/wiki/Crimes_2.0 | 2026-07-17]
- Crime "Hustling" (Fraud, enhancer Megaphone): gather an audience, demo the confidence trick, get them betting, deliberately lose small to bait bigger bets, then cash in - but the more you win, the more suspicious they get, so they bet less or leave. 0 unique outcomes. [source: https://wiki.torn.com/wiki/Crimes_2.0 | 2026-07-17]
- Crime "Disposal" (Illicit Services, enhancer Latex Gloves): 12 job categories, each with 3-5 disposal methods that use different amounts of nerve and sometimes consume items - pick the right method by intuition/experience. Only a limited number of jobs per day; once the day's jobs are done, nothing more until tomorrow. 11 unique outcomes. Honors: reach level 100, dissolve a dead body in acid, and clear all the day's jobs. [source: https://wiki.torn.com/wiki/Crimes_2.0 | 2026-07-17]
- Crime "Cracking" (Cybercrime, enhancer Office Chair): brute-force passwords character by character using computer resources; you can guess unencrypted characters, but 3 wrong guesses locks you out of that password. Start with a Computer/Laptop; at level 25 you unlock rig chassis you kit out with CPU and cooling to raise MIPS (more characters cracked per cycle). No unique outcomes. Honors: reach level 100, and guess 250 password characters. [source: https://wiki.torn.com/wiki/Crimes_2.0 | 2026-07-17]
- Crime "Forgery" (Counterfeiting, enhancer Magnifying Glass): craft fake items from raw materials - 14 possible: Driver's License, Parking Permit, Concert Ticket, Diploma, Birth Certificate, License Plate, Skeleton Key, Prescription, ID Badge, Travel Visa, ATM Key, Bank Check, Police Badge, Passport. No unique outcomes. Honors: reach level 100, and have 10 projects on cooldown at once. A crit fail can lose your printer. [source: https://wiki.torn.com/wiki/Crimes_2.0 | 2026-07-17]
- Crime "Scamming" (Fraud, enhancer Ergonomic Keyboard): collect email addresses and launch spam waves hoping for responses; a bigger address database unlocks more complex, better-paying scams. Three education courses help your scamming. No unique outcomes. Honors: reach level 100, and respond to a victim's Temptation, Sensitivity, Concern, and Hesitation within one successful scam. [source: https://wiki.torn.com/wiki/Crimes_2.0 | 2026-07-17]
- Crime "Arson" (Vandalism, enhancer Windproof Lighter): set fires that behave differently by target, your actions, and materials used; targets vary by size, flammability, and rurality, and jobs range from simple burn-and-collect to complex requirements for bigger rewards. 16 unique outcomes. Honors: reach level 100, destroy the largest building type in under 30 seconds, and use all fifteen ignitors, accelerants, and dampeners. [source: https://wiki.torn.com/wiki/Crimes_2.0 | 2026-07-17]
