- Companies (overview): companies are player-owned businesses run by a director (the owner). You need to be level 3 to work in a company and level 10 to own one. The director hires employees, sets their pay, orders stock, prices goods, budgets advertising, and keeps the company vault funded for weekly payroll and ads. Companies were released in 2007. [source: https://wiki.torn.com/wiki/Company | 2026-07-17]
- Company ranking (stars): companies have a 10-star ranking based on gross profits, updated automatically every Sunday. Stars matter a lot: each star gives 1 job point per day to the director and each employee, and the director's daily working-stat gain scales with stars - 1 star = 5 units/day, 2 stars = 10, 3 stars = 20, 4 stars = 35, and 5+ stars = 50 units/day. [source: https://wiki.torn.com/wiki/Company | 2026-07-17]
- Company metrics: Popularity = your weekly income as a % of the top-earning company of the same type (comparative only, no direct income effect). Efficiency = average employee performance, ideally 100%, updated daily around 18:00 TCT. Environment = a score from staff-room size, employee count, and conditions; drug use and inactivity lower it, cleaners and managers raise it, and low environment can cut income by up to a third. [source: https://wiki.torn.com/wiki/Company | 2026-07-17]
- Company employee effectiveness: driven by your working stats vs the job's requirements, a Settled-In bonus (+1 per 24h, max +10 after 10 days), the director's business education bonuses, the Employee Effectiveness merit (+1 per point), and dragged down by drug addiction and inactivity. [source: https://wiki.torn.com/wiki/Company | 2026-07-17]
- Company special positions: Cleaner (boosts environment), Manager (raises underperforming employees' effectiveness), Marketer (improves advertising budget effectiveness), Secretary (detailed employee earnings breakdown), and Trainer (generates 0.1 trains per 10 effectiveness). [source: https://wiki.torn.com/wiki/Company | 2026-07-17]
- Company advertising: caps at a 40% base effect and is reduced by rival companies' ad budgets. It can be boosted by a Marketer employee, the Marketing (BUS2400) and Media Law (LAW2100) education courses, and the TGP stock benefit. [source: https://wiki.torn.com/wiki/Company | 2026-07-17]
- Company applications: you can have up to 10 job applications open at once; they expire after 72 hours if not actioned. After a decline there's a 72-hour reapply wait (a director can override it), and accepting an application auto-cancels your other pending ones. [source: https://wiki.torn.com/wiki/Company | 2026-07-17]
- Company job points & specials: directors and employees earn job points daily at 18:00 TCT (1 per company star). Job Specials are perks spent from (or passively given by) job points; some need a minimum star rating to unlock, and energy-granting specials are capped at 100 job points per day. [source: https://wiki.torn.com/wiki/Company | 2026-07-17]
- Company upgrades: Size upgrades add +25% employee capacity each (up to 6, costing 10%-60% of startup each). Staff Room upgrades keep environment up (up to 7 tiers, 2.5%-15% each). Warehouse upgrades add storage (up to 7, 5%-30% each). The final Size and Staff Room upgrades require the director to have completed Bachelor of Commerce (BUS3130). [source: https://wiki.torn.com/wiki/Company | 2026-07-17]
- Company employee training & pay: directors can train employees (+50 to the primary work stat, +25 to secondary) and set pay up to a $25,000,000 cap. Each day around 18:00 TCT the company closes out - tallies customers and gross income, applies the director's stat gains, hands out job points, runs payroll, and generates employee trains (can take ~15 min to show). [source: https://wiki.torn.com/wiki/Company | 2026-07-17]
- Selling a company back: if you sell a company to the system you get 75% of its cost (including 75% of any upgrades you bought) plus 5% of the total revenue it made over its lifetime. [source: https://wiki.torn.com/wiki/Company | 2026-07-17]
- Director education perks (companies): +2% productivity each from Statistics (BUS2200), Corporate Finance (BUS2500), Logistics (BUS2800), Business Ethics (BUS2100), and E-Commerce (BUS2120); +5% employee effectiveness from Communication (BUS2300) and +7% from Corporate Strategy (BUS2600); pricing power from Pricing Strategy (BUS2700, +10%) and Product Management (BUS2900, +5%); advertising from Marketing (BUS2400) and Media Law (LAW2100); and Bachelor of Commerce (BUS3130) unlocks the top upgrade tiers. [source: https://wiki.torn.com/wiki/Company | 2026-07-17]
